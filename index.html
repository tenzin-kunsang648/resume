About me

Currently pursuing a master's degree in computer science with a focus on Artificial Intelligence and Data Science, I blend my expertise in mathematics, software development, and data engineering to delve deep into the nuances of computer vision, machine learning, and natural language processing. With three years as a full-stack developer, I have spearheaded data-driven projects, integrated complex databases with intuitive front-end interfaces, applied exploratory statistical analysis and employed machine learning algorithms to refine data schemas and enhance strategic decision-making. My work included lots and lots of writing technical documentations for stakeholders and technical architects.

In tandem, I've served as a mentor to junior developers, guiding them through the complexities of code and analytical thought and best practices, and volunteered my knowledge in high-school level mathematics to uplift the Tibetan community through Yindayin. As a secretary for the Regional Tibetan Women's Association in Minneapolis, I contributed my organizational and design skills to drive initiatives such as providing scholarships to some of the brightest minds in the Tibetan community. My professional ethos and personal philosophy are rooted in leveraging data and sharpening intuition - to evolve through continuously learning and application of our knowledge for the good of our communities, no matter the scale of our impact.

During my leisure hours, I enjoy devling into my internal world through writing poems and proses, reading books, practicing yoga, and listening to music. I also enjoy hiking, spending time with my friends and family, exploring new places in familiar surroundings, and watching films —both the masterpieces and the not-so-great ones, especially when I'm in the mood for something less mentally taxing. These days, I am drawn to films that embrace the silence and feelings and slowness similar to "The Banshees of Inisherin", "Frances Ha", and "Past Lives".
